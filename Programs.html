COOKING PROGRAM

HOW DOES IT WORK?
STEP 1
Planning meeting
The members gather to plan a menu around advertised specials in major food store flyer's. Each participant pays for his or her share of the food. This is calculated by dividing the total cost of the food by the number of servings required

STEP 2
Cooking session The members get together at a designated kitchen to prepare and cook the recipes. At the end of the cook, each member has several meals to take home and freeze for later, thus furthering healthy eating habits. Members share recipes, nutritional knowledge as they cook and everyone gains from each other’s enthusiasm. Some participants learn new recipes and are introduced to new foods contributing to a more balanced diet.

BIRTHDAY PROGRAM

Mandate: Bringing hope, joy and a sense of self-worth to homeless children with the gift of a birthday party.For a child experiencing homelessness, something as simple as a birthday party can seem miles out of reach. These children live under a blanket of stress, worry, depression, and sacrifice. On a daily basis, they go without.
These children are who we serve.
The child’s information is sent to us through children’s advocates, caseworkers and counselors through various forms of shelters and other programs.
Birthday-in-a-Box
In many cases, group birthday parties are not viable, so we provide a Birthday-in-a-Box. Birthday boxes are centered around a fun theme that is consistent with the child’s interests. A typical box has the following:
• Cake / Candles
• Decorations
• Party Game
• Gifts
• Birthday Card
• Juice and Cups / Juice Boxes
• Goody Bags
• Plastic Utensils
• Themed Napkins
• Themed Plates
• Party Hats
• Balloons
• Tablecloth
• Birthday Banner

Kindle-Kiddies Cotton wear

Kindle kiddies Cotton is a preowned clothes up cycling program, which SOURCES clothing, shoes, bedding, curtains, fashion accessories and other textile items from schools and various community individuals.
All sourced clothing and other textile items are processed by our up cycling partners, who are individuals from all around the world with a passion for sewing and knitting. Items are sorted and categorized to go into one of the following streams:
Pre-loved clothing, shoes, fashion accessories , and other textile items in good condition is hand crafted into beautiful products like rompers with adjustable straps to pants with hand-knitted chords, with hand-carved wood and plastic buttons, to our Easypeasy Long Sleeve jerseys, calf-high booties made from soft breathable cotton:
All items are handmade and come with the name of the artisan who made them, to provide a unique signature. On top of this, all our knits are hypoallergenic, seamless, and knitted with love and passion, to bring warmth and comfort to children of all age’s.

KKM Soccer Club Programs
Adult Rec League
We are very excited to be the first organization to host a community Recreational Soccer League. Every year there are three (3) Divisions: A Division (18+) is intended for competitive or skilled players who still play at a high level; B Division (18+) is for players of varying ability and competitiveness; while C Division (35+) is for first time players, or players looking to play in a social, fun, and recreational setting with no contact. Registration Rules and Procedures
1. A Division (18+)* is intended for competitive and/or skilled players.
2. B Division (18+)* is for players of varying ability, competitiveness, and experience.
3. C Division (30+)* is for players looking to play in a social, fun, and recreational setting.
4. Priority guarantee of a spot in the league is determined by the date that both the registration form and donations are received by the Club Registrar. A player is only considered REGISTERED once they have submitted a completed form and has donated towards KKM food or sanitation programs. If donation is provided by cheque then the date that donation is received by the Club Registrar will be considered the registration date.
5. Team Captains may submit a roster to the League Convener. Teams must have a minimum of 12 players listed on the roster, or wait-listed players will be placed on teams by the League Convener.
6. The players that are listed on the team roster will be placed on the desired team ONLY if they: A) indicate the team name on the registration form, and
B) register and donate prior to the league reaching the maximum registered.
7. No player will be placed on a team until they are registered. Only registered players are allowed to play in any league game. Any team captain that allows a non-registered player to play for their team assumes full liability for any injuries caused or suffered by the non-registered player.
8. Teams submitting a roster of 8-10 players must have a minimum of 3 female players listed; teams submitting a roster of 11-14 players must have a minimum of 4 female players listed.
9. Any player who registers after the league has reached the maximum number of registered players will be placed on a wait list regardless of whether or not they are listed on a team roster.
10.UNDERAGE PLAYERS* ("C" Division only): Players under the age of 30 may be rostered in "C" Division at the discretion of the league conveners to a maximum of 2 per team. "C" Division is intended for players of varying ability and who wish to play in a social, fun, and recreational setting. Players with exceptional ability may be moved to the "A" or "B" Divisions to maintain league parity.
11.Carded Players MUST play in "A" Division to maintain league parity. * Women may play in the division of their choosing, regardless of age.
